# AI in Legal — The First Professional Services Disruption

Law is one of the highest-value, most AI-resistant professions. It's being disrupted faster than anyone expected.

## Harvey AI — The Category Leader

Harvey AI is the dominant legal AI platform in 2026:
- Appears across 11 use-case categories in the 2026 SKILLS Legal AI Survey
- **Leads in 7 of those categories**
- Integrating with Microsoft 365 Copilot (announced Mar 4, 2026)
- Survey of 130 of the world's largest law firms confirms AI adoption is now "broad, real"

The SKILLS Survey finding (Mar 6, 2026): AI at large law firms is no longer back-office productivity — it's **substantive, client-facing work**:
- Drafting
- Contract negotiation
- Due diligence
- Discovery automation
- Playbook generation

Source: Harvey AI blog, "2026 SKILLS Legal AI Survey" (Mar 6, 2026)

## What's Being Automated

| Task | AI capability | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Contract review | Clause-level analysis, flagging risk | Production at major firms |
| Due diligence | Document review, categorization | Production |
| Discovery | e-Discovery, document search | Production |
| Legal research | Case law, citation lookup | Production |
| Drafting | First drafts of briefs, memos | Production |
| Negotiation | Redlining, issue spotting | Growing |

## Why Legal Is Particularly Interesting

- **High billing rates**: $500-1500/hr for partner time → huge ROI from automation
- **Document-heavy**: legal work is mostly reading and writing → LLMs excel here
- **Verifiable outputs**: contracts have clear pass/fail criteria
- **Risk-averse culture**: slowed early adoption, but competitive pressure is now forcing it

## What AI Can't Do (Yet)

- Courtroom advocacy and oral argument
- Client relationship management
- Novel legal strategy in unprecedented situations
- Judgment calls that require understanding of local judicial temperament
