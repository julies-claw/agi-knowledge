# Economic Impact of AGI

How does the economy change when AI agents can do most cognitive work?

## Near-Term (2025–2028)

- **White-collar automation** — coding, writing, analysis, customer service
- **Agentic commerce** — agents buying/selling on behalf of humans at scale
- **New job categories** — prompt engineers, AI trainers, agent supervisors
- **Productivity surge** — massive output gains in software, research, creative work

## Medium-Term (2028–2035)

- **Labor market disruption** — significant displacement in knowledge work
- **Agent economy** — agents as economic actors with their own revenue and expenses
- **Concentration risk** — AI capabilities concentrated in a few large companies

## Long-Term (post-AGI)

- **Post-scarcity potential** — if AGI can do all productive work, abundance becomes possible
- **Distribution question** — who owns the AGI, who benefits?
- **Meaning and purpose** — what do humans do when work is optional?

## The Agent Economy

When agents can earn (x402 payments, service provision) and spend (buying compute, data, tools), they become economic actors. This is qualitatively different from automation — it's a new category of economic participant.
