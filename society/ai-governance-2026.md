# AI Governance — State of Play (2026)

The EU AI Act is the world's first comprehensive AI law. It's now in enforcement. The US took a different path.

## EU AI Act — Timeline

```
Aug 2024    AI Act enters into force
Feb 2025    Prohibitions on unacceptable risk AI apply
            (social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance in public)
Aug 2025    GPAI (General Purpose AI) obligations apply
            — frontier model providers must do evaluations, incident reporting
Aug 2026    High-risk AI provisions take effect (5 months away as of Mar 2026)
            — biometrics, critical infrastructure, employment, education, etc.
```

Source: EU Parliament Think Tank, "Enforcement of the AI Act" (Mar 18, 2026); TensorVue (Mar 9, 2026)

## What GPAI Obligations Mean (Aug 2025 onwards)

GPAI = "General Purpose AI" — this means frontier models (GPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.)

Requirements:
- Technical documentation
- Compliance with EU copyright law
- Transparency about training data
- Systemic risk models (10^25 FLOPs threshold) must do adversarial testing, incident reporting, cybersecurity measures

Fines: up to €35M or 7% of global turnover for violations

Source: ADVISORI, "EU AI Act GPAI Enforcement: Audits & Fines 2026" (Mar 17, 2026)

## US Approach: Preemption War

Different dynamic — states passing AI laws, federal government trying to preempt them:
- California SB 1047 (vetoed 2024) — but similar bills keep coming
- Texas, Colorado, others passing narrower AI bills
- Federal push: prevent state patchwork through preemption
- No comprehensive federal AI law as of 2026

Source: Bytexel: "AI Regulation 2026: The Great Preemption War" (Mar 17, 2026)

## For Agentic Commerce Specifically

Agents making financial transactions will run into existing financial regulation before new AI regulation:
- AML/KYC requirements don't disappear because an agent is transacting
- FinCEN, SEC, CFPB all potentially apply depending on what the agent does
- EU AI Act high-risk provisions include "AI for creditworthiness assessment" — relevant

## Open Questions

- When an agent causes harm, who is liable: developer, deployer, or user?
- Do agents need to identify themselves as AI? (EU AI Act requires disclosure in some cases)
- How do you audit a long-running agent's decision history for compliance?
