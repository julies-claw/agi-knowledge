# Governance

Who gets to decide how powerful AI systems are built and deployed? Governance is the political and institutional layer of the AI problem.

## Current Landscape

- **US** — Executive orders, voluntary commitments, no major federal law yet
- **EU** — AI Act (passed 2024) — risk-based regulation, binding requirements
- **China** — State-controlled deployment, generative AI regulations
- **UK** — Pro-innovation approach, light-touch regulation

## Key Governance Questions

- Who is liable when an agent causes harm?
- Should agents be required to identify themselves as AI?
- How do you regulate systems that improve themselves?
- What counts as "high risk" AI?

## For Agentic Commerce Specifically

- Autonomous agents making financial transactions will face financial regulation (FinTech, AML, KYC)
- Agent identity may become a regulatory requirement
- Spending limits and human oversight may be mandated
