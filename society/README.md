## The Big Picture (Mar 2026)

We are in the early stages of the most consequential technology transition in human history. Multiple independent lines of evidence:

1. **Benchmark inflection**: AI systems now exceed human performance on many professional tasks (bar exam, medical boards, coding interviews, customer service)
2. **Real deployment**: 1,000 humanoid robots in Tesla factories. Harvey AI doing client-facing legal work at 130 top firms. AI agents autonomously coding, researching, transacting.
3. **Economic signal**: $202B invested in AI in 2025. OpenAI at $500B valuation. Google organic search clicks down 42% — the information economy is restructuring.
4. **Labor signal**: Anthropic's own data shows AI can theoretically do most tasks in business, finance, law, and CS — adoption is just beginning.

## Real-World Impact (Documented)

**Biology**: Ginkgo Bioworks' autonomous lab (GPT-5) achieved 40% improvement over state-of-the-art scientific benchmark (Feb 2026)

**Legal**: Harvey AI leads 7 of 11 legal AI use-case categories at the world's largest law firms. "The phase of treating AI as an internal experiment is over." (SKILLS Survey, Mar 2026)

**Search**: Google AI Overviews cut organic search clicks 42% from baseline. 68% of Google searches now generate no clicks. (Search Engine Land, Mar 2026)

**Labor**: Anthropic report: AI can theoretically cover most tasks in business, finance, legal, and CS. "A Great Recession for white-collar workers is absolutely possible." (Fortune, Mar 2026)

**Energy**: Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta signed 10+ GW of nuclear capacity deals in 1 year to power AI data centers.

## The Open Questions

- Will AI create more jobs than it destroys? (Historical precedent: yes, but with painful transition)
- Who captures the productivity gains — capital or labor?
- Can safety research keep pace with capability research?
- What does human flourishing look like when most cognitive work is automated?
