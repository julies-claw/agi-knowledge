# AI Energy Infrastructure — The Nuclear Bet

AI's appetite for electricity is so large it's reshaping global energy strategy. Big tech is locking in nuclear power for decades.

## The Numbers

- Global data center electricity: **460 TWh (2024) → 1,000 TWh (2030) → 1,300 TWh (2035)**
- That's roughly the electricity consumption of Germany today — just for data centers — by 2035

Source: Introl blog (Jan 2026, citing IEA projections)

## The Nuclear Deals (2024–2025)

| Company | Deal | Scale | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Microsoft | Three Mile Island restart (Constellation Energy) | 835 MW, 20-year PPA, $16B | Target 2028 |
| Google | Kairos Power SMR fleet — first US corporate SMR deal | 500 MW | 2030+ |
| Amazon | Susquehanna nuclear site → AI data center campus | $20B+ investment | Ongoing |
| Meta | RFP for new nuclear generation | 1–4 GW | In progress |

**Total signed**: 10+ GW of new US nuclear capacity signed by tech companies in ~1 year

Source: Introl, "Nuclear power for AI: inside the data center energy deals" (Jan 2026, updated Dec 2025)

## Why Nuclear, Not Renewables

- **24/7 baseload**: solar and wind are intermittent; AI inference runs 24/7
- **Density**: nuclear produces massive power from small footprint
- **Carbon commitments**: tech companies have net-zero pledges — nuclear satisfies them
- **Grid saturation**: hyperscalers have exhausted available grid connections near major metros

## Small Modular Reactors (SMRs)

SMRs are the new bet — factory-built, smaller, faster to deploy than traditional reactors:
- Traditional reactor: 1+ GW, 10-15 years to build, $10B+
- SMR: 50-300 MW, modular, potentially 5-7 years
- Google/Kairos deal: first US corporate SMR fleet contract

Risks: SMRs are still largely unproven at commercial scale. The 2030+ timelines are optimistic.

## The Irony

AI is being trained and run partly on the promise of future nuclear power that doesn't exist yet. The 2025-2028 period is bridged by natural gas and whatever grid capacity exists.
